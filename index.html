01 · who, briefly

Designing for the people that health posters and shop signs and tech apps usually forget about.

I trained in Marketing and Interactive Design, which is a fancy way of saying I argue a lot about the message and the kerning in the same afternoon. 02 · about

Growing up South Asian in Australia, I developed an early appreciation for the beauty of cultural nuance.

Frequent travels to India and abroad transformed this appreciation into a lifelong curiosity about how identity is expressed and maintained. I went from designing Western Fusion outfits and experimenting with international flavours in the kitchen to designing strategically for inclusivity, especially for the underrepresented communities.

My experience in Marketing and Interactive Design allows me to approach problems from multiple perspectives, bridging the gap between business strategy and human-centric design. Being bilingual has given me the unique ability to navigate diverse communities and advocate for those often labelled as 'difficult' or 'misrepresented' in traditional design frameworks.

I am dedicated in creating user-centered design thinking to prototype solutions that resonate across different demographics.

My passion for Marketing and social media has taught me that connection is a universal human need. I strive to build platforms where everyone feels seen, designing for the marginalised voices.

When creating digital spaces in Figma my goal is to ensure digital environments are as diverse, nuanced, and accessible as the people who inhabit them. I design for the misunderstood, striving to build a more inclusive future one user experience at a time.

By merging my cultural insights with design methodology, I transform the complexities of human identity into intuitive digital experiences that drive both social progress and user resonance.

I work with restaurants, clinics, retirement communities, and the occasional games cabinet. My background in marketing means I think about the message and the kerning in the same afternoon.

The work here spans print, UX, web and interactive design. Each brief is different. The approach is always the same: understand the people first, then design.
